Diagnosis: Plantar fasciitis

Technique: After obtaining informed consent, the patients fasica muscle(s) were palpated for the painful area of complaint.

The following was done:

A soft recess just distal to the lateral maleoli was palpated. The area was marked at the injection site with the pen tip in order to leave an impression in the skin. The skin was thoroughly cleaned with betadyne then alcohol. Performed with the patient in a seated position with the ankle supinated. A anterolateral approach was used, under ultrasound guidance with color dopplar on the 25g 1 1/4 inch needle was advanced into the distal medial calcanius region, into the palntar fascia. Color dopplar used to ensure that no vessels were penetrated. Special care was made to avoid all vessels. Aspiration was done to make sure vessels were not penetrated. After negative aspiration, the full contents of the syringe was injected, and then band-aid was applied.

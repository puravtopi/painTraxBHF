Chief Complaint: Low back pain

Diagnosis: Sacroiliac pain, Sacroiliitis

Procedure: (Side) Sacroiliac joint injection with ultrasound guidance

Description Of Procedure: The patient was identified and was found to be stable. Because the patient reports that the pain still continues despite conservative care, and given the MRI findings and physical exam, the indication for the procedure persists.

The patient was put in the prone position. The medication combination below was drawn using a 22 gauge 1 ½ inch needle. The injection field was prepped with Betadine three times. The skin and subcutaneous tissues were anesthetized with 1% lidocaine, with a skin wheal was showing. A 22 gauge spinal needle was then advanced to the (side) sacroiliac joint under ultrasound guidance. The interspace between the sacrum and the illum was identified clinically and verified via ultrasound guidance technique. Ultrasound was used to guide the needle into the intra-articular joint space and negative aspiration of blood was confirmed. Subsequently an intra-articular injection into the SI joint was performed under sterile technique. At this time the following mixture of medication was injected in the joint:

A 3 cc solution of mixed with 1cc of 2% lidocaine, and 2 cc of 6 mg of Celestone, was then injected into the joint.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was discharged without complications. This should stand for the letter of medical necessity for the requested procedure.

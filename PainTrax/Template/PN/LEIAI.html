PREOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS : Left Elbow internal derangement.

POST OPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS : Left Elbow internal derangement.

PROCEDURE: Intra-articular left elbow injection of steroids with Ultrasound Guidance.

INDICATIONS: Radiohumeral joint injection was used to diagnose and treat the painful and swollen elbow due to rheumatoid arthritis or nonspecific inflammatory arthritides and trauma related pain and inflammation. The patient was identified and was found to be stable. Because the patient reports that the pain still continues, and given the findings and physical exam, the indication for the procedure persists.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE: After informed consent was obtained, the patient was placed in the sitting position with the elbow flexed to 90 degrees. The lateral epicondyle and posterior olecranon are palpated. The patient was prepared in a standard aseptic fashion over an area large enough to allow palpation of landmarks, and sterile technique was used throughout the procedure. Bony landmarks were identified clinically and verified via Ultrasound guidance technique.

A 1½-in., 25-gauge needle was inserted into the groove just above and lateral to the olecranon process, just below the lateral humeral epicondyle and posterior to the head of the radius. The needle was gently manipulated into the joint. Ultrasound was used to guide the needle into the intra-articular joint space and negative aspiration of blood was confirmed.

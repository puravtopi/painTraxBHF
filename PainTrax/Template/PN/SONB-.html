Procedure Report: (Side) supraorbital nerve blocks under ultrasound guidance

Name:#name Date:#date
Location:#location DOB:#date

Diagnoses : 1. Chronic Headaches.

2. (side) Ocular Pain.

The procedure was performed under ultrasonic guidance. The (side) supraorbital foramen was located, 2-3 cm lateral to the midline of the face, at the inferior edge of the supraorbital ridge. A 25g 1" needle was slowly introduced at a perpendicular angle immediately superior to the (side) supraorbital notch. The needle was then withdrawn 1-2 mm to avoid intraneural injection. Ultrasonic guidance was utilized for needle placement imaging supervision and interpretation. Examination of the bony structure and other soft tissue, revealed no abnormalities. There was no evidence of intravascular injection at the site. Following negative aspiration for blood, a mixture of 40mg DepoMedrol and 2cc of 0.25% Marcaine preservative free was injected with visual confirmation of fluid spread around the target.

The same procedure was done on the opposite side.

COMPLICATIONS: None.

PROCEDURE Note: Right De Quervains Tenosynovitis injections under ultrasound guidance

Name:#name Date:#date
Location:#location DOB:#date

Diagnosis: Myofascial pain syndrome, M65.4 radial Styloid tenosynovitis

Technique: After obtaining informed consent, the patients muscle(s) were palpated for the painful area of complaint.

The following was done:

The area was marked at the injection site with the pen tip in order to leave an impression in the skin — Point of maximal tenderness (on distal, lateral/radial side of radius). The skin was thoroughly cleaned with alcohol. The patient was sitting with the radial side of the wrist up. With the tuberculin needle/syringe, the skin was entered at about 20-30-degrees to the skin. Under ultrasound guidance with color dopplar on, the needle was advanced until it was confirmed that needle was under the tendon sheath of the abductor pollicis longus (APL) and extensor pollicis brevis (EPB), but NOT in the actual tendons. Special care was made to avoid all vessels. Aspiration was done to make sure vessles were not penetrated. After negative aspiration, the full contents of the syringe was injected, and then band-aid was applied.

The injectate included the following mediations.


_0.5_cc of 1% lidocaine

_0.5_cc of 40mg/cc of depomedrol


The patient tolerated the procedure well with no adverse events.

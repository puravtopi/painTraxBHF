Procedure Note: Right Knee Pes Anserine bursa joint injection under ultrasound guidance

Name:#name Date:#date
Location:#location DOB:#date

CLINICAL HISTORY: Right knee Pes Anserine bursitis

DIAGNOSIS: Right knee Pes Anserine bursitis (M70.51)

ANESTHETIC AGENT: 0. 1% lidocaine.

MEDICATION: 40mg Depo-Medrol.

NEEDLE UTILIZED: 1.5 inch 25 gauge, 3ml syringe.

Medical Necessity:

The patient complains of moderate, right inframedial knee pain more around the pes anserine bursa region. On a scale of one to ten the patient rates the pain as 8. The patient has found temporary and minimal relief with conservative treatment. The patient's physical examination is consistent with Pes Anserine bursitis. It is indicated at this time for the patient to undergo a Right Pes anserine bursa injection in an attempt to restore them to their prior functional capacity and comfort .

The patient was placed in seated with his legs hanging. The patient was then prepared in the usual sterile fashion using alcohol and Chlorascrub. Using a transducer and sterile ultrasound gel The transducer was place in the transverse plane approximately two inches medial and inferior to the right knee patella. The transducer was used to identify the tibial tuberosity, and the pes anserine bursa. Fluid was noted in the bursa. With ultrasound guidance, a 25 gauge 1 1/2 inch needle was put into the right pes anserine bursa, with minimal fluid aspirated. After that fluid was discarded, another 3 cc syringe was used to inject 2cc of 1% lidocaine and 1cc of 40mg of depomedrol into the pes anserine bursa.

There were no paresthesias or complaints reported by the patient. The patient was then discharged to the chiropractor for treatment. The patient was advised to continue with their conservative treatment such as home exercise, physical therapy and chiropractic treatment. The patient was given an appointment to follow up in 1 week and to notify the office immediately if fever or signs of bleeding or discomfort arise.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was discharged without complications. This should stand for the letter of medical necessity for the requested procedure.

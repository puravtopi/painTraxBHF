Diagnosis:

Technique: After obtaining informed consent, the patient was seated in an upright position. The (side) rectus femoris muscle was cleansed with alcohol prep pad.

The following was done:

The medication combination below was drawn up in 3 mL sterile syringe with a 27 gauge one and a half inch sterile needle. The syringe was held at a 90° angle and advanced into the (side) rectus femoris muscle . The plunger was aspirated and no blood was drawn back into the syringe. The plunger of the syringe was pushed forward to inject content of syringe into the (side) rectus femoris muscle. Needle and syringe were retracted from the (side) rectus femoris. A bandage was placed over the injection site.

The injectate included the following mediations.

_1_cc of 30 mg of toradol _1_ cc normal saline

The mixture was injected after aspiration was negative for blood or air. The ultrasound machine was also used to visualize the medication going into the muscles to avoid any vulnerable areas such as arteries, veins and nerves.

The patient tolerated the procedure well with no adverse reaction or complaints of pain.
